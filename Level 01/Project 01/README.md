Retail Sales Data Analysis - Project 01
📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a retail sales dataset. The primary goal is to identify consumer purchasing behaviors, analyze sales performance over time, and provide actionable insights for business growth. This project was developed as part of a Data Analytics internship.

📊 Key Findings & Insights
Based on the analysis conducted in the notebook:

Sales Trends: Sales increased steadily in the first half of 2023, reaching a peak between May and July.

Top Categories: Electronics generated the highest total revenue ($156,905), followed closely by Clothing ($155,580).

Customer Demographics: Female customers contributed a slightly higher share of total sales ($232,840) compared to male customers ($223,160).

Demand Balance: Purchasing demand is well-distributed, though Clothing attracts the highest number of unique customers (351).

🚀 Key Analysis Steps
Data Loading & Cleaning: * Checked for null values and duplicates.

Converted date columns to datetime objects and optimized data types for numerical analysis.

Descriptive Statistics: * Calculated mean, median, mode, and standard deviation for Age, Quantity, Price, and Total Amount.

Time Series Analysis: * Resampled data to visualize monthly sales trends using Matplotlib and Seaborn.

Customer & Product Segmentation: * Analyzed total customers and revenue across different product categories.

Analyzed sales distribution based on gender.

Visualization: * Line plots for sales trends.

Bar charts for category and gender comparisons.

Heatmaps to show the correlation between Gender and Product Category revenue.

🛠️ Tech Stack
Language: Python

Libraries: * Pandas: Data manipulation and cleaning.

NumPy: Numerical computations.

Matplotlib & Seaborn: Data visualization and trend analysis.
